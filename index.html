I am a second-year Comparative Literature student at King’s College London (KCL).

I reside in the UK, but grew up with an international background between Beijing, Washington D.C., and London, which gives me a unique global outlook. As of now, I am passionate about academic topics related to contemporary literature, translation, cross-cultural studies, and international relations.

I was named a California Arts Scholar upon completing the Creative Writing programme at California State Summer School for the Arts (CSSSA), and my work was recognised as one of the top 60 entries by the BBC Young Writers’ Award in 2023. I am currently committed to my role as the President of King’s College London Vocaloid Society since establishing it in 2024, where I'm responsible for character/graphic design, and social media marketing amongst other things.

Feel free to reach out to me by emailing klin20049@gmail.com (in English/Chinese中文/Japanese日本語), or find out what writing/translation projects I've been up to on my personal blog!

    I founded and am leading as the President of Vocaloid Society until September 2026, a culture and music appreciation society focused on vocal synthesis technology, the largest student community of its kind in the UK.

    In the 2025-26 academic year, I led a committee of 5 who were a great help with in-person event setup, but I was practically responsible for everything outside of event risk assessments (which I did up until September). I’m currently in charge of planning/organising events (including collaborations), managing an active social media presence of 1,000+ followers across English and Chinese platforms, including a Discord community of 350+ members, and a YouTube channel that has accumulated 10k+ views; I’m responsible for almost all of the society posters/graphics on Instagram, as well as some of the illustrations.

    I also established external partnerships with vocal synthesis industry professionals, for example, by hosting a talk with Dreamtonics Co., Ltd. employee Yuka Wong, and facilitated national media exposure through coverage on the NHK (Japan Broadcasting Corporation)’s documentary NEO-JAPONISM, on the episode J-POP: Vocaloid Goes Global (link accessible until July 5, 2026), broadcasted in Japan and internationally.

    More information about KCL Vocaloid Society can be found on their KCL Student Union page, or their official website. Some major events are listed separately on this page.

    UK university-wide Project Sekai: Colorful Stage! tournament, hosted under KCL Vocaloid Society. Special thanks to Warwick Vocaloid Society for handling technical aspects of the livestream (specifically Rohan T. for designing the stream interface and Wilson F. for designing the tournament format), and the unofficial Imperial Rhythm Games Society/City, University of London’s Gacha Society for assisting with the event as co-hosts.

    Project Sekai is a rhythm game that features Vocaloid songs (Japanese pop/rock songs made using voice synthesiser softwares), which can be played using a phone or tablet. This tournament consisted of two rounds – one preliminary round held online on Discord, and the final round, held in person at B5 Auditorium, King’s College London Waterloo Campus (capacity 300), which the top 16 players of the preliminary online round advanced into.

    The final round was open to spectators from any and all universities and featured three performances, with prizes awarded to the best three players at the very end. It was also streamed online, with the YouTube livestream gaining 1000 views in real time.

    Credits:
    Main visual/promotional poster: illustration by Tsunamayy
    Photos: Wilson Fung, Rohan Tantepudi and others

    The London Anime Union Halloween Party was a large scale collaborative event (with a turnout of 200+ in total) that took place in KCL Strand Campus’s flagship venue, the Great Hall. It was co-hosted by KCL Vocaloid Society and 5 Anime Societies across London universities: KCL, UCL, Imperial College London, SOAS and City, University of London. The event consisted of society stalls with games, a quiz about anime/Japanese pop-culture, a cosplay catwalk, and a cosplay competition where prizes were awarded. A Chinese vlog was edited by the main photographer Gavin Xie after the event.

    There was a photo booth at the event, where attendees (who were encouraged to come in cosplay) could have their pictures taken and printed by the main photographer. The Great Hall was decorated with Halloween-themed ornaments, as well as anime-style drawings/posters, in compliance with university regulations (as the venue was a heritage listed site). The event achieved a net profit of 246.9%, which was used to cover costs reimburse contributors.

    Despite KCL Vocaloid Society being the newest member of London Anime Union, I immediately took on the role of the leader, managing a total of 30+ committee members involved. I was awarded Volunteer of the Month by KCLSU for overseeing the planning (including venue coordination) and onsite logistics of this event.

    Credits:
    Main visual/promotional poster: illustration by JrHanaba, graphic design by Doodler
    Photos: Gavin Xie and Utku Kaplan
    Cosplay Catwalk organisation: SOAS Anime Society
    Quiz design: UCL Anime Society

    I presented how Vocaloid Society members volunteered to make cultural initiatives like this event happen at the KCLSU Volunteer Showcase (photo credit: KCLSU).
